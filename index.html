Monthly Ticket Volume by Origin

"Services" (NPI, deployments) consistently drive the majority of ticket volume, peaking in January/February before decreasing. "CARE" (operational support) tickets remain a smaller, yet fluctuating, stream of requests, highlighting different demand patterns between new projects and ongoing support.

Ticket Priority Distribution

A significant portion of tickets are high-priority, with 'Blocker' and 'Critical' issues indicating frequent, impactful service disruptions for customers and pointing to underlying platform complexities.

Geographical Insights: Ticket Volume vs. Fault Rate

North America generates the highest ticket volume, likely due to a large user base. However, Europe and the Middle East & Africa exhibit a much higher fault percentage, indicating that issues from these regions are more often genuine product failures rather than user queries.

Top 10 Customers by Total Ticket Volume

T-Mobile entities dominate the ticket volume, with the main USA corporation submitting a significantly higher number of tickets than any other customer. This highlights the T-Mobile account as the primary driver of support workload, followed by other key accounts like AT&T and M1.

Analysis of Common Issue Types

Issues related to software upgrades (66 tickets) remain the top driver of support requests. Problems with Storage/Ceph (34) and Certificate Management (34) are tied as the next most frequent issues, followed closely by Networking (29) and Deployment/Installation (18).

Root Cause of Support Requests by Origin

While simple "Queries" are a major driver of requests, "Competency/Human Error" is a substantial issue across both CARE (18 cases) and Services (49 cases). Issues with "Other Products/Infrastructure" are also a significant factor, especially for Services, pointing to integration challenges.

Deep Dive: Competency-Related Issues

"Incorrect configuration" is overwhelmingly the largest contributor to competency-related tickets for both CARE and Services teams. This suggests a critical need for more robust, user-friendly configuration guides, validation tools, and targeted training to reduce common human errors during deployment and maintenance.

Support Origin Deep Dive

Analyzing the nature of requests from CARE versus Services reveals critical differences in their support profiles, highlighting distinct challenges and opportunities for improvement in each area.

Fault Rate by Origin

CARE tickets have a higher probability of being a genuine "Fault" (14.6%) compared to Services tickets (10.7%), suggesting that operational issues are more likely to be critical system failures.

Overall R&D Engagement Rate

R&D engagement is higher for CARE tickets (24%) than for Services (20%). This indicates that post-deployment, operational issues are more likely to require deep, specialized expertise from the development team.

R&D Engagement on Support Requests Only

Focusing only on non-fault tickets, R&D engagement is nearly identical for Services (12%) and CARE (11.3%), indicating a consistent need for deep expertise on non-fault escalations across both workstreams.

R&D Engagement on High-Priority Tickets

For the most severe tickets (Blocker & Critical), R&D engagement for Services (30%) is higher than for CARE (24%), suggesting that high-impact issues during NPI and deployments are more likely to require R&D intervention.

Strategic Recommendations

Target Core Issues

Launch dedicated programs to re-engineer and automate certificate management and software upgrade processes, which are recurring themes in ticket data.

Enhance RCA & Training

Enforce mandatory RCA for all "Faults". Address the high volume of "Competency" errors with targeted user and partner training.

Strengthen NPI Support

Allocate more resources for PRE-CARE and early-life support, and improve knowledge transfer from R&D to frontline teams to reduce escalations.
